BMI is a measurement of a person's leanness or corpulence based on their height and weight, and is intended to quantify tissue mass. It is widely used as a general indicator of whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends BMI categorization for children and teens between age 2 and 20.Health professionals have been using body mass index, or BMI, to help decide whether people are overweight or underweight for more than 100 years.
